# Executive Overview

**Core premise (short):** A world once tuned by meta-agents called Architects (Celestials) is now facing the "Long Descent." To prevent a catastrophic reset, a lowly archivist named Maer steals the *Codex of the First Breath* and the Azure Shard. As the **Vault-Keeper**, he must collect five Shards of reality and climb the Zenith to shatter the system and return magic to the world below, even if it costs him his humanity.

**Tone:** epic, mythic, but intimate. Systems-minded magic with human consequences. Repeated prismatic imagery; Shard-Resonance motif; sensory Ruination (Name-Bleed).

---

# Cosmology & Core Concepts

## The Mortal Face & The Meridian Veil

- **Mortal Face**: The observable world — land, sea, cities, living beings.
- **Meridian Veil**: The metaphysical strata regulated by the Celestials.
- **The Great Filter / Val-Kora**: The primary antagonist. Val-Kora is a Celestial who enforces the "Edict of Names" and the quarantine of the world.

## The Shards of Inheritance

The world's power is concentrated into five Shards, each representing a fundamental aspect of reality:

1. **Azure Shard (Mind):** Located in the Virek God-Engine. Grants intelligence but causes insomnia and erasure of common nouns.
2. **Veridian Shard (Life):** Located in the Deep Weald. Grants healing but causes loss of the memory of pain and recklessness.
3. **Crimson Shard (Body):** Located in the Ashlands. Grants physical strength but causes loss of empathy and personal memories.
4. **Violet Shard (Entropy):** Located in The Hollow. Grants power over decay but turns the holder "Hollow."
5. **Gold Shard (Law):** Located in Cyris. Allows rewriting of reality.

---

## The Vanishing & The Fracture

- **Canonical origin (chosen truth):** The *Fracture* — an event in Year 907 R — occurred when an Arc-Foundry (Virek) test using a Mercury-Keel prototype attempted to temporal-shift a node in the Treaty of Three Stones crossing. The node’s feedback resonated with Architect-patterns. The The Great Filter activated network-wide, sealing architects’ direct signatures from the Mortal Face. Architects were either cut off, sealed, or retreated to the Veil. This coordinated activation is what cultures call the Vanishing.

# Magic Systems & Rules

## Magic Taxonomy & Signatures

Sources of magic and their signature costs/visuals:

1. **Spirit (Thalen)**  
   - *Cost:* memory erosion, empathy drain  
   - *Visual:* green-gold prismatic sap shimmer  

2. **Covenant / Codex (Cyris)**  
   - *Cost:* rights and social obligations; legal bindings  
   - *Visual:* marble light, white fissure lines  

3. **Engineered / Technomancy (Virek)**  
   - *Cost:* material consumption, mechanical strain  
   - *Visual:* brass sparks and schematic overlays  

4. **Tidal / Deep (Kallum)**  
   - *Cost:* pieces of self, seasonal debts  
   - *Visual:* oily iridescence, slow breathing waves  

5. **Void-Edge / Anti-ontology (Nyth)**  
   - *Cost:* identity slippage, conceptual erosion  
   - *Visual:* absence-consuming hues, silence-bloom

## Leylines, Nodes, and Entropy

- **Leylines** are flows of Meridian current. **Nodes** are concentrated nodes/off-ramps.  
- **Entropy model (narrative formula):** Repeated use increases node entropy E(N) non-linearly. Above thresholds, quarantine triggers may occur. Entropy is not easily reversed.

**Narrative rule:** Magic is a resource with cumulative, irreversible local consequences.

## Ascension — The Three Immutable Laws

1. **Irrecoverable Cost:** An ascension attempt permanently consumes a fragment of the caster (a proper name, memory, or lineage marker). Irretrievable.

2. **Node Entropy:** Ascension draws massive energy from nodes and permanently raises their entropy. Repeated or large ascensions risk quarantine.

3. **Political Targeting:** Unsanctioned ascension provokes enforcement by institutions and may trigger Great Filter response. It cannot be done in isolation.

## Left-hand Rites & Inversion Mechanics

- **Left-hand rites** invert the moral/contractual economy of magic: instead of giving or pledging, they borrow, extracting a function of the Meridian temporarily and returning it with interest (usually a memory or future cost). These rites are culturally taboo because they parasitize trust.

# Institutions, Factions & Politics

## Major Institutions (with motives & internal factions)

1. **Codex Courts (Cyris)**  
   - *Motive:* Containment of Shard radiation; prevent the Great Filter from engaging.  
   - *Internal:* Moderates (containment & safety) vs Hardliners (total lockdown of all arcane activity).

2. **Verdant Cirri (Thalen)**  
   - *Motive:* Mitigation of Name-Bleed via ecological symbiosis.  
   - *Internal:* Elders (traditional containment) vs Youngers (symbiotic mutation).

3. **Arc-Foundry (Virek)**  
   - *Motive:* Synthesis of Shard energy for industrial power.  
   - *Internal:* Engineers (focus-shielding research) vs Profiters (weaponization of raw code).

4. **Tide-Ledger Houses (Kallum)**  
   - *Motive:* Control of "Arcane Fallout" in sea lanes.  
   - *Internal:* Conservators (guarding fallout-records) vs Smugglers (trafficking Shard residue).

5. **Archivum Noctis**  
   - *Motive:* Documentation of the world before the "Blank Slate" occurs.  
   - *Internal:* Preservationists (archiving memories) vs Liberators (seeking a cure for Name-Bleed).

6. **White Hand (cult)**  
   - *Motive:* Ritualistic mutation; becoming one with the Shard energy.  
   - *Internal:* Idealists (ascension seekers) vs Fanatics (those who force Name-Bleed on others).

**Narrative dynamics:** Alliances and betrayals among these groups create political urgency; all have human, sympathetic motives.

# Deep Regional Histories & Daily-Life Profiles

*(Use these one-page profiles as chapter-openers to ground scenes.)*

## Cyris — *Marble Dominion* (Containment & Law)
- **Imagery:** white terraces and stone steps, geometric containment seals carved into courtyard walls.  
- **Daily life:** Silence Zone patrols in the morning; magistrates inspecting focus-shields; children learn the "Safe-Code" hand signs.  
- **Food & smell:** salt-baked bread, cured fish; ozone and crushed chalk.  
- **Rituals:** Seal-Check (daily verification of home dampening fields).  
- **Great fear:** a breach in the containment vault that triggers Name-Bleed.  
- **Festival:** Day of Shielding — families reinforce their protective sigils publicly.

## Thalen — *Verdant Confluence* (Symbiosis & Root)
- **Imagery:** tree-cities, living bridges grown over Shard-residue nodes.  
- **Daily life:** monitoring Name-Bleed in the forests; crafts grown to filter arcane fallout.  
- **Food & smell:** sapbread, fermented roots; earthy, metallic hum.  
- **Rituals:** Root-Grafting (planting trees to absorb radiation).  
- **Great fear:** the grove succumbing to Shard-Hunger.  
- **Festival:** Bloom-Shield — community prayers for stable biology.

## Virek — *Brass Ascendancy* (Synthesis & Power)
- **Imagery:** towers of brass, steam-smoke, conveyor bridges humming with artificial energy.  
- **Daily life:** clockwork shift rhythm; arc-patent queues; markets buzzing with synthesized Shard-tech.  
- **Food & smell:** oily stews, metal-sweet solder; ozone.  
- **Rituals:** Shield-Testing; engineers invoke arc-credits.  
- **Great fear:** a reactor meltdown causing mass cognitive erosion.  
- **Festival:** The Great Ignition — demonstration of new synthesis tech.

## Kallum — *Saltbound Expanse* (Shard-Hunger & Scavenging)
- **Imagery:** lead-lined docks, tide-marked fallout zones.  
- **Daily life:** tide-timed scavenging for Shard residue; fallout-trackers at piers.  
- **Food & smell:** smoked fish and brine; bone-polished wood and the reedy hum of Name-Bleed.  
- **Rituals:** Ash-scattering to "ground" the mind before sea travel.  
- **Great fear:** a Shard-Storm rising from the deep.  
- **Festival:** The Grounding — night of communal silence to reset neural pathways.

## Eirn — *Frostbound Oathlands* (endurance & pacts)
- **Imagery:** glacial halls, etched stone oaths.  
- **Daily life:** clan hearths, slow chores, oaths carved in ice.  
- **Food & smell:** warm broth, preserved fatcakes.  
- **Rituals:** Oath-endurance (ice-breath tests).  
- **Great fear:** frozen lineages from broken pacts.  
- **Festival:** Stone-Hold Day — clan pacts renewed.

## Sahru — *Sunken Wastes* (cautionary ruin)
- **Imagery:** black sands, glass-forged trade plazas.  
- **Daily life:** ash-markets; people avoid lasting names.  
- **Food & smell:** scant preserved roots; dry wind.  
- **Rituals:** Communal silence at midday to reduce revenant calls.  
- **Great fear:** Black Sands and Name-Bleed.  
- **Festival:** Memory-Fast — day of silence.

## Alkyr — *Starward Climb* (pilgrimage & listening)
- **Imagery:** sheer cliffs, cliffside shrines, ropes.  
- **Daily life:** pilgrims descend/ascend; small alt-shops.  
- **Food & smell:** dried fruit, altitude tea.  
- **Rituals:** listening vigils for Meridian winds.  
- **Great fear:** Star-drowning — losing self to Veil.  
- **Festival:** Sky-Murmur — community vigils.

## Mirel — *Shrouded Coast* (mist & echo)
- **Imagery:** reedy coasts, lamp-lit mist markets.  
- **Daily life:** barter storytelling; mist-sellers.  
- **Food & smell:** smoked eel, damp wood.  
- **Rituals:** Mist-weaving — veiling names.  
- **Great fear:** Return-Echoes (waking with pieces of other peoples' memories).  
- **Festival:** Echo-Night — lamps for returned names.

## Nyth — *Fracture Beyond* (anti-ontology)
- **Imagery:** jagged rocks, impossible angles; research encampments.  
- **Daily life:** researchers and riskers; few permanent residents.  
- **Food & smell:** hard biscuit; sterile water.  
- **Rituals:** disbelief practice; counter-chants.  
- **Great fear:** losing the concept of “I.”  
- **Festival:** none — science symposia occasional.

## Valen — *Central Plain* (hub & crossroads)
- **Imagery:** broad plains, market crossroads, caravan rings.  
- **Daily life:** constant trade, rumor-swapping; inns full of travelers.  
- **Food & smell:** spiced stews; dust.  
- **Rituals:** Caravan-lit pledges for safe routes.  
- **Great fear:** node ruptures that sever trade.  
- **Festival:** Cross-Trade week — merchants exchange tales & sigils.

# Lore Compendium — Long Entries

*(These can be used as codex entries or epigraphs.)*

## The Fracture — Full Account (canonical)

> In the year 907 of the Reckoning, the Treaty of Three Stones convened its engineers, ritualists, and archivists upon a crossing of leylines in the Central Plain. The Arc-Foundry’s Mercury-Keel prototype, designed to “phase” a node for temporal observations, was calibrated to a new range. That night the node sang not a local song but an old pattern—an Architect-signature. The Great Filter awoke in a way that had not happened for an age. Locks spread like a cold net, sealing nodes, cutting flows, and severing Architects from their Mortal work. The world felt like a house whose doors had been slammed suddenly. What men called the Vanishing is the echo of that slam. Cities changed, treaties collapsed, and the Meridian became a guarded thing. The Hollow’s Seal was keyed that night.
>
> — *Codex Courts, Fracture Dossier, annotated*

## The Hollow Chronicle (excerpt / full)

> The Hollow was never the shrine of a single Aspect. The altar sits upon a triune helix of glyphs that, when whole, would have permitted careful bridging among Cyclewright and Binder currents. The Book that floats there was created by an anonymous archivist who sought synthesis: a manual to reconcile law with living memory and with the mechanical schematics of inventors. They stitched methods together and then wrote a final line across the binding: *“We stitched what we could not pay for.”* When the Fracture’s Lock tripped, the Book became quarantine-chamber and key. It holds patterns both to re-tune a node and to burn it clean. How that can be both cure and contagion is the core caution written into the Hollow’s architecture.
>
> — *Archivum Noctis fragment; Hollow Field Notes*

## The First False Ascension (Sahru) — full

> The city of Zur-Sahru crowned King Haraes in 968 R. Haraes felt the itch of legend: to sit among Ascended Kings whose names were carved into the sun-washed steps of history. In 972 R he obtained stolen components: a Pillar-slate from Cyris, a star-shard from Alkyr’s black market, and a living tongue from a Thalen grove. He attempted Ascension alone, believing the gods undone and that mortal will could take the mantle. The ritual failed. Flesh brightened to glass; the tide of names left the ground in a breath. The surrounding ten miles lost permanent naming: infants could not keep given names; words slid off tongues. King Haraes became a revenant — something that remembered himself only as hunger for names. The Black Sands were born; a warning that would remain for generations in children’s rhymes.
>
> — *The Sahru Annals of Loss*

## The Treaty of Three Stones — Canonical text (excerpt)

> *Signed, Year 102 R, on the Central Plain.*  
> Representatives from Cyris, Virek, and Thalen hereby agree to joint stewardship of the Crossing Node. We acknowledge that no single polity shall monopolize node access. A rotating Codex Tribunal shall adjudicate access under the terms of binding — and all parties shall maintain a ledger of activity to be stored in triplicate.
>
> — *Treaty of Three Stones, translated*

## Overview

- **Name:** Maer.
- **Title:** The Vault-Keeper.
- **Role:** Former Archivist who stole the seeds of the world's rebirth.
- **Burden:** Cumulative Name-Bleed from the five Shards.

## The Shards & Their Costs

As the Vault-Keeper, Maer's body and mind are the containment vessels for the Shards. Each Shard grants immense power but exacts a devastating toll.

- **Azure Shard (Mind):** 
  - *Power:* Intelligence spike, pattern recognition.
  - *Cost:* Insomnia, erasure of common nouns.
- **Veridian Shard (Life):**
  - *Power:* Rapid healing, organic growth control.
  - *Cost:* Green veins, loss of memory of pain (recklessness).
- **Crimson Shard (Body):**
  - *Power:* Physical strength, armor-skin.
  - *Cost:* Loss of empathy, erasure of loved ones' memories.
- **Violet Shard (Entropy):**
  - *Power:* Control over decay and gravity.
  - *Cost:* Becoming "Hollow"—cold and detached.
- **Gold Shard (Law):**
  - *Power:* Rewriting reality, projecting truth.
  - *Cost:* Burning of all remaining personal identity.

- Lean into the moral paradox: power as burden, knowledge as both ladder and razor. The refrain *“Mortals should not meddle with the power of the Gods”* should be omnipresent — sometimes as law, sometimes as proverb, and sometimes as accusation.
