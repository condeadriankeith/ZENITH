A compassionate but weary healer from the floating groves of Thalen, Isala uses "Spirit-Sap" and mountain herbs to treat the physical and metaphysical wounds caused by the Fracture. She is a woman of quiet strength and deep empathy, her hands always stained with the iridescent oils of her trade.

Isala is a woman of middle age, her skin a deep, warm bronze etched with fine lines of laughter and sorrow. She wears a heavy, multi-pocketed apron over a simple linen tunic, each pocket containing a different vial, poultice, or bandage. Her hair is a silver-grey braid, often adorned with dried flowers. She carries a small, brass "Distillation-Kit" and a bag of "Sap-Sponges." She smells of crushed mint, lavender, and the sharp, antiseptic scent of pine-resin.

Isala serves as a source of healing and emotional support. She provides the Traveler with the "Sap-Poultices" needed to treat the "Name-Bleed" and helps them understand the human cost of the world's decay.

The soft, rhythmic *clink* of her glass vials and the gentle, calming hum she uses while she works.

## Relationship to World Shards
- **Motivation:** Isala treats "Name-Bleed" as a terminal illness. She seeks to find a cure for the cosmic radiation Maer is emitting.
- **Goal:** Harvest "Shard-Sap" from the Veridian aspect to create a stabilizer for Maer's mind.

## Moral Contradictions
- She is a healer who uses the very poison she fights to create her medicines.
- She wants to save Maer, but she is secretly documenting his "dissolution" for the High Peaks Archive.

### Supplemental Lore: The Sap-Healing of Thalen

In the floating groves of Thalen, medicine is a form of "Energetic Alignment." The "Spirit-Sap" that flows through the Great Trees is a highly resonant fluid that can be used to stabilize and "tune" the human nervous system.

The Sap-Healers are trained to identify the "Dissonances" caused by the Fracture—the physical and mental imbalances that occur when a person's internal resonance is out of sync with the world's Meridian Lattice. By applying different types of sap and herbal poultices to specific "Node-Points" on the body, a healer can restore the patient's balance and help them resist the effects of the Name-Bleed. However, the process is not without risk. The healer must "absorb" a portion of the patient's dissonance into their own body to neutralize it, a practice that can lead to "Resonance-Fatigue" and even permanent mental instability.

### The Distillation-Kit of Isala

Isala’s kit is a masterpiece of "Bio-Alchemical" engineering.

1. **The Sap-Condenser:** A series of glass tubes and copper coils used to concentrate the spirit-sap.
2. **The Herbal-Grinder:** A mortar and pestle made of resonant wood, used to infuse the sap with mountain herbs.
