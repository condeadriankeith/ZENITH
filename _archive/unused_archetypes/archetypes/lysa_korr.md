Lysa Korr — The Spirit-Weaver

Slug: lysa_korr.md
Region: Thalen

A young but powerful Shaper from the floating islands of Thalen, Lysa communicates with the lingering spirits of the land to "grow" the world's defenses. She views the Fracture as a sickness of the world's memory that can only be healed through partnership and song.

Lysa is lithe and graceful, her skin a deep olive tone decorated with swirling patterns of glowing green-gold sap. She wears simple tunics made of woven grass and living vines that slowly change color throughout the day. Her hair is a wild mane of dark curls, often adorned with bioluminescent flowers. She carries a "Spirit-Staff" made from a single, un-severed root of the Great Tree. She smells of wet earth, crushed mint, and the sweet, heavy scent of Thalen lilies.

Lysa acts as a guide to the world's emotional and spiritual landscape. She helps the Traveler understand the "Name-Bleed" and provides the "Spirit-Vines" needed to bridge the gaps in the crumbling reality.

The faint, melodic humming that seems to follow her and the way the ground-moss rises to meet her feet as she walks.

## Relationship to World Shards
- **Motivation:** Lysa believes the Veridian Shard is crying out. She sees Maer not as a thief, but as a wounded carrier who needs the Shaper's guidance to avoid being consumed by the "Root-Rot" of the Shard.
- **Goal:** Guide Maer through the Thalen groves and teach him how to "pulse" the Veridian Shard without losing his mind.

## Moral Contradictions
- She preaches harmony with the Shards, but she uses a "Name-Binding" vine to keep her own memories from bleeding away.
- She loves the world, but she would let it burn if it meant the Veridian Shard survived the Great Filter.

- Act I — Ch. 8 (The Floating Groves): Lysa saves the Traveler from a name-hungry revenant and introduces them to the concept of "Spirit-Partnership," showing them how to listen to the world's pain.
- Act II — Ch. 20 (The Weeping Canopy): Lysa and the Traveler work together to "heal" a minor node, a process that requires Lysa to absorb a portion of the node's corruption into her own spirit.
- Act III — Ch. 28 (The Final Song): Lysa leads a chorus of spirits to hold back the "Absence" while the Traveler enters the Hollow, her own form beginning to turn into translucent spirit-sap.

As she witnesses the horrific transformations caused by the Fracture and is forced to destroy corrupted spirits she once loved, she loses her youthful innocence. ### Supplemental Lore: The Shapers of Thalen

Shaping in Thalen is a form of "Collaborative Creation." Unlike the Engineers who impose their will on matter, or the Magistrates who impose their will through law, Shapers enter into a "Spirit-Contract" with the land. They provide the intent and the melody, and the spirits provide the growth and the form.

This process is mediated by "Spirit-Sap," a highly resonant fluid that flows through the Great Trees of Thalen. By coating their skin in this sap, Shapers can "synchronize" their nervous systems with the local ecosystem, allowing them to feel the health of the nodes and the movements of the spirits. However, this connection is two-way. If the land is in pain, the Shaper feels it. If the node dies, a part of the Shaper dies with it.

### The Spirit-Staff of Lysa

Lysa’s staff is a "Living Relic." It is a branch of the First Tree that has never been severed from its source. It exists simultaneously in the physical world and the spirit-mirror. 

1. **The Bloom-Tip:** A cluster of flowers that glow brighter when near a healthy node.
2. **The Root-Grip:** A series of tendrils that wrap around Lysa's arm, allowing her to draw energy directly from the land.
