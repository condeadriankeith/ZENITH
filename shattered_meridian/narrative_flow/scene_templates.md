# Scene Templates

## Standard Scene Template

### Header Information
```
# Scene Title: [Scene Name]
**Location:** [Specific location in the world]
**POV:** [Point of view character]
**Time:** [Time of day, season, chronology reference]
**Mood/Atmosphere:** [Brief description of the scene's emotional tone]
```

### Opening
- **Setting Description:** Ground the reader in the specific location with sensory details
- **Character Placement:** Show the POV character's state and situation
- **Hook:** Engage the reader with conflict, mystery, or compelling action

### Development
- **Action/Dialogue:** Advance the scene's immediate goal
- **World Building:** Integrate relevant aspects of the setting, magic system, or culture
- **Character Development:** Reveal character traits, emotions, or relationships
- **Thematic Resonance:** Connect to larger themes of the work

### Conclusion
- **Stakes Elevation:** Increase tension or reveal consequences
- **Transition:** Lead into the next scene or section
- **Cliffhanger/Revelation:** End on a note that propels the reader forward

## Magic System Scene Template

### Setup
- **Type of Magic:** Identify which magic taxonomy is being used (Spirit, Covenant, Engineered, Tidal, Void-Edge)
- **Cost Calculation:** Establish what the character will pay for this use
- **Visual Manifestation:** Describe the specific visual/auditory/tactile elements

### Execution
- **Method:** How the magic is performed (ritual, gesture, incantation, etc.)
- **Effect:** What the magic actually accomplishes
- **Side Effects:** Any unintended consequences or additional costs

### Consequences
- **Immediate:** What happens right after the magic is used
- **Long-term:** How this use affects entropy, political targeting, or other systems
- **Integration:** How the magical event connects to the larger plot

## Regional Scene Template

### Cultural Integration
- **Local Customs:** Show the region's unique practices and beliefs
- **Environmental Details:** Use the region's specific geography and climate
- **Social Structure:** Reflect the region's governance and social organization

### Magic Integration
- **Regional Magic Type:** Utilize the area's predominant magical approach
- **Local Resources:** Reference the region's unique magical materials or knowledge
- **Practitioner Types:** Include local experts or practitioners

### Thematic Alignment
- **Regional Theme:** Connect to the themes most prominent in this region
- **Character Growth:** Use the region's challenges for character development
- **Plot Advancement:** Move the overall story forward through regional elements

## Character-Driven Scene Template

### Internal State
- **Current Emotion:** Show the character's psychological state
- **Motivation:** Establish what drives the character in this scene
- **Conflict:** Present internal struggles or competing desires

### External Action
- **Goal:** What the character is trying to achieve
- **Obstacle:** What prevents easy accomplishment
- **Method:** How the character attempts to overcome challenges

### Character Growth
- **Change:** Show how the character is different after the scene
- **Learning:** What insight or knowledge is gained
- **Relationships:** How character connections evolve

## Action Scene Template

### Setup
- **Stakes:** What will happen if the protagonist fails
- **Environment:** How the setting affects the action
- **Capabilities:** What each participant can bring to the conflict

### Confrontation
- **Escalation:** How the conflict intensifies
- **Tactics:** The strategies employed by each side
- **Turning Points:** Moments that shift the balance of power

### Resolution
- **Outcome:** Who succeeds and who fails
- **Costs:** What was sacrificed to achieve the result
- **Consequences:** How this affects the larger story

## Dialogue Scene Template

### Purpose
- **Information Transfer:** What information is conveyed
- **Character Development:** How relationships or personalities are revealed
- **Plot Advancement:** How the conversation moves the story forward

### Dynamics
- **Power Balance:** Who has control in the conversation
- **Subtext:** What is really being discussed underneath the surface
- **Tension:** What underlies the spoken words

### Style
- **Voice:** Each character's distinctive speech patterns
- **Pacing:** How the rhythm affects the scene's mood
- **Integration:** How dialogue fits with action and description

## World Building Scene Template

### Discovery
- **New Information:** What the reader learns about the world
- **Integration:** How this connects to previously established elements
- **Implications:** What this means for the story or characters

### Demonstration
- **Active Elements:** Show how world elements function in practice
- **Rules Application:** How the world's systems actually work
- **Consistency:** Ensure alignment with established lore

### Immersion
- **Sensory Details:** Help the reader experience the world
- **Cultural Depth:** Show the richness of the world's societies
- **Believability:** Make the fantastical feel real and lived-in

## General Guidelines

### Canon Compliance
- Verify all canonical phrases appear where required
- Ensure magic system rules are followed consistently
- Check that place names and character traits remain accurate
- Confirm that the Three Laws of Ascension are respected

### Thematic Consistency
- Each scene should advance at least one major theme
- Symbolic elements should be used appropriately
- Motifs should be maintained throughout

### Continuity Management
- Track entropy accumulation across scenes
- Monitor the Traveler's growing political targeting
- Maintain consistency in character relationships and development
- Keep timeline and chronology accurate
