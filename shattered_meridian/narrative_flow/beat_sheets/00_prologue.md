# Prologue Beat Sheet

## Core Beats

### Approach
- **Setting:** The Hollow, a ruined sanctuary with an altar at its center
- **Atmosphere:** Desolate, wounded space with stone ribs overhead and glass-fiber dust
- **Character Introduction:** The Traveler arrives, nameless and carrying his pack of knowledge
- **Objective:** To retrieve a name through interaction with the floating Book

### Personalization
- **Emotional Anchor:** Reference to the mother's name traded for the valley
- **Backstory Element:** The lullaby that has lost its last verse
- **Personal Stakes:** The debt he carries from previous bargains
- **Character Details:** Left-handed nature and the scar on his wrist

### Touch
- **Sensory Details:** Copper taste in the air, reedy well song, prismatic light
- **Physical Interaction:** Palm to the binding, the Book's response
- **Magical Manifestation:** Light braiding into his arm, violet and gold
- **Immediate Consequence:** The tolling bell and rising well tone

### Warning
- **Ominous Message:** The stones speak the canonical refrain: "Mortals should not meddle with the power of the Gods"
- **Escalating Risk:** The Lock registers the exchange and nodes respond
- **Foreshadowing:** Beneath the town, a node remembers the mortal's touch
- **Final Image:** The Traveler walking out with dangerous knowledge

## Scene Structure

### Opening Image
The Hollow as a wound in the world, with light threading through cracks like failed prayers.

### Set-up
The Traveler approaches the altar and the floating Book with its orbiting shards of arcana.

### Catalyst
He places his palm on the binding, initiating contact with the forbidden.

### Debat
The Book responds with light and sound, indicating the gravity of his transgression.

### Break into Two
Despite the warning, he continues, driven by his need for the name.

### B Story
The emotional subplot of his mother's traded name and the missing lullaby verse.

### Fun and Games
The magical exchange occurs, with prismatic light and the Book's song.

### Midpoint
The stones speak the canonical warning, marking the point of no return.

### Bad Guys Close In
The Lock registers the exchange; nodes beneath the town respond.

### All Is Lost
The surge is painful, and he feels a hollowing as a small word drops away.

### Dark Night of the Soul
He withdraws his hand with only a fragment, questioning his choices.

### Break into Three
He decides to continue despite the risks and consequences.

### Finale
He departs with the stolen scrap, carrying the dangerous knowledge.

### Final Image
The Traveler walking back into thin light, burdened with the lullaby's ache.

## Required Elements

### Canon Compliance
- The canonical refrain must appear verbatim: "Mortals should not meddle with the power of the Gods"
- The Fracture context should be subtly referenced
- The Three Laws of Ascension should be implied through consequences

### Character Consistency
- The Traveler must be portrayed as nameless and left-handed
- His pack of knowledge should be mentioned
- The emotional anchors must be referenced
- The left-hand motif should be present

### World Building
- The Meridian Lock system should be demonstrated
- Magic should manifest with prismatic/oil-on-water imagery
- The entropy consequences should be shown
- Regional flavor of the Hollow should be established

## Narrative Function

### Exposition
Introduces the world's magic system, the consequences of meddling, and the Traveler's unique position.

### Character Introduction
Establishes the Traveler's motivation, methods, and the personal costs he bears.

### Thematic Statement
Demonstrates the central theme of the cost of power and the tension between individual need and universal law.

### Plot Initiation
Sets up the central conflict between the Traveler's goals and the world's restrictions.
