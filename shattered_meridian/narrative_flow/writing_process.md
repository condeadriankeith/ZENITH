# Writing Process Guide

## Pre-Writing Phase

### 1. Research and Preparation
- **Review Canon:** Read the [Shattered Meridian Compendium](../compendium/shattered_meridian_compendium.md) to ensure consistency
- **Check Planning Docs:** Consult the [Narrative Master](narrative_master.md), [Character Arcs](character_arcs.md), and [Theme Map](theme_map.md)
- **Verify Requirements:** Confirm canonical phrases, place names, and character constraints
- **Review Previous Work:** Look at recent scenes for continuity

### 2. Scene Planning
- **Select Scene:** Choose from the [Scene Index](scene_index.md) that needs drafting
- **Define Goals:** What must this scene accomplish for plot, character, and theme?
- **Identify Constraints:** Which canonical elements must be included?
- **Outline Structure:** Brief outline using the [Scene Template](scene_templates.md)

## Writing Phase

### 3. Draft Creation
- **Follow Template:** Use appropriate scene template from [Scene Templates](scene_templates.md)
- **Maintain Voice:** Keep consistent tone with cinematic, sensory, lean prose
- **Apply Motifs:** Consistently use left-hand motif and prismatic imagery
- **Check Canon:** Ensure canonical phrases appear verbatim where required

### 4. Integration Requirements
- **Magic System:** Apply rules from [Systems Arcana](../systems/systems_arcana.md) consistently
- **Character Traits:** Maintain consistency with [Traveler Bible](../characters/traveler.md)
- **World Elements:** Respect established geography and cultures
- **Thematic Content:** Align with themes from [Theme Map](theme_map.md)

## Revision Phase

### 5. Content Review
- **Canon Compliance:** Verify all canonical elements are present and correct
- **Continuity:** Check consistency with previous scenes and established lore
- **Character Consistency:** Ensure character behaviors align with established traits
- **Thematic Integration:** Confirm thematic elements are properly incorporated

### 6. Technical Review
- **Prose Quality:** Maintain cinematic, sensory, lean style without purple prose
- **Dialogue:** Ensure all dialogue serves plot, character, or thematic purposes
- **Pacing:** Verify appropriate rhythm for scene type and story position
- **Description:** Balance sensory details with narrative momentum

## Quality Assurance Phase

### 7. Validation Checklist
- [ ] Canonical phrases appear verbatim in required locations
- [ ] Place names match canonical list exactly
- [ ] Character traits remain consistent
- [ ] Magic system rules followed without contradiction
- [ ] Three Laws of Ascension respected
- [ ] Left-hand motif and prismatic imagery present
- [ ] No exposition paragraphs exceed 400 words
- [ ] Name-Bleed sensory hint present where required

### 8. Peer Review Preparation
- [ ] Scene follows [Scene Template](scene_templates.md) structure
- [ ] Scene integrates with [Chapter Template](chapter_template.md) if applicable
- [ ] Scene advances plot according to [Narrative Master](narrative_master.md)
- [ ] Scene contributes to character development per [Character Arcs](character_arcs.md)

## Submission Phase

### 9. Pre-Submission Review
- **Final Proofread:** Check for grammar, spelling, and style issues
- **Link Verification:** Ensure all internal links function correctly
- **Length Check:** Verify scene length is appropriate for purpose
- **Hook Effectiveness:** Confirm opening and closing are compelling

### 10. Documentation Updates
- **Scene Status:** Update the [Scene Index](scene_index.md) to reflect completion
- **Progress Tracking:** Update any relevant planning documents
- **Continuity Notes:** Document any new elements that affect future scenes
- **Revision Log:** Note any significant changes from original plan

## Style Guidelines

### Tone and Voice
- **Cinematic:** Create vivid, film-like scenes that readers can visualize
- **Sensory:** Emphasize tactile, visual, auditory, and other sensory details
- **Lean:** Avoid overly elaborate or flowery prose
- **Consistent:** Maintain voice throughout the work

### World Building Integration
- **Show Don't Tell:** Demonstrate world elements through action and interaction
- **Consistency:** Maintain established rules, cultures, and magic systems
- **Relevance:** Ensure all world elements serve the scene's dramatic purpose
- **Immersion:** Help readers feel present in the world

### Character Development
- **Authentic:** Characters should behave consistently with their established nature
- **Progressive:** Each scene should advance character development in some way
- **Motivated:** Character actions should stem from clear, believable motivations
- **Distinct:** Each character should have a recognizable voice and personality

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### Canon Violations
- Never contradict the Three Laws of Ascension
- Don't alter established place names or character traits
- Avoid creating new region/place names outside the canonical list
- Don't ignore the consequences of entropy or political targeting

### Prose Issues
- Avoid lengthy exposition paragraphs (keep under 400 words)
- Don't overuse purple prose or overly elaborate descriptions
- Avoid inconsistent use of motifs and recurring elements
- Don't neglect the left-hand motif and prismatic imagery

### Character Problems
- Don't let the Traveler use his right hand for bindings when left hand is required
- Avoid forgetting the Traveler's nameless, left-handed, pack-carrying characteristics
- Don't ignore the emotional anchors and their impact on motivation
- Don't allow inconsistent magical abilities

### World Building Errors
- Don't introduce new magic types not covered in the taxonomy
- Avoid ignoring entropy consequences of magical use
- Don't create new factions or institutions without planning
- Don't contradict established regional characteristics
