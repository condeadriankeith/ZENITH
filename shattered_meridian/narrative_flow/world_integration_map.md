# World Integration Map

## Chapter 1 Integration Map

### Lore & Canonical Strings
- **Name-Bleed Hints:** 
  - Scene 1.1: The village well hums a steady, reedy tone upon the Traveler's birth.
  - Scene 1.4: "Name-Fever" in the village serves as an early manifestation of local node instability.
- **Ascension Laws:** 
  - Law 1 (Irrecoverable Cost) is demonstrated in Scene 1.4 (Kael’s memory loss) and Scene 1.5 (trading the mother's face for knowledge).
- **The Fracture:** 
  - Referenced in the mentor's dialogue as the reason for the world's "bite" and the necessity of the Hollow quest.

### Artifacts vs. Exposition
- **Cradle Ledger Bone (Scene 1.1):** Replaces exposition about village naming customs with a physical blockquote entry.
- **Midwife's Scrap (Scene 1.1):** Provides sensory context for the birth omen through marginalia.
- **Village Ledger Margin Note (Scene 1.2):** Establishes the economics of naming debts in Valen/Cyris culture.
- **Ledger-Bone Exchange Note (Scene 1.5):** Documents the trade of memory for knowledge, reinforcing the concept of memory as currency.
- **Mentor’s Scribbled Hint (Scene 1.5):** Provides the first clue to the Hollow without lengthy background explanation.

### Geographic & Cultural Seeds
- **Valen/Cyris Influence:** The nameless valley uses naming rituals and ledgers, seeding the legalistic culture of Cyris and the crossroads nature of Valen.
- **White Hand Rumors:** Subtle seeds of radicalism mentioned in Scene 1.3 as the elders watch the forbidden left-hand experiment.

## Geographic Integration

### Region-Specific Elements
Each region serves both as setting and as thematic representation:

#### Cyris - The Marble Dominion
- **Magic System:** Covenant / Codex magic focused on legal bindings
- **Cultural Elements:** Oath recitations, public ledgers, Door-oath rituals
- **Narrative Function:** Demonstrates the value of social contracts and legal frameworks
- **Integration Points:** Codex Courts, Pillar of Oaths, marble light visuals
- **Thematic Role:** Represents order and the power of binding agreements

#### Thalen - The Verdant Confluence
- **Magic System:** Spirit magic emphasizing partnership with living systems
- **Cultural Elements:** Song exchanges, naming ceremonies, growing crafts
- **Narrative Function:** Explores the relationship between memory and identity
- **Integration Points:** Grove Speakers, Naming rituals, green-gold prismatic visuals
- **Thematic Role:** Represents growth and the preservation of organic connections

#### Virek - The Brass Ascendancy
- **Magic System:** Engineered / Technomancy focused on precision and mechanism
- **Cultural Elements:** Clockwork rhythms, arc-patents, prototype demonstrations
- **Narrative Function:** Examines the relationship between innovation and safety
- **Integration Points:** Arc-Foundry, Mercury-Keel prototype, brass spark visuals
- **Thematic Role:** Represents progress and the double-edged nature of advancement

#### Kallum - The Saltbound Expanse
- **Magic System:** Tidal / Deep magic involving memory commerce
- **Cultural Elements:** Tide-timed trade, ledger-writers, bone offerings
- **Narrative Function:** Investigates the economics of memory and the value of names
- **Integration Points:** Tide-Ledger Houses, Salt-Verse, bone-polished visuals
- **Thematic Role:** Represents preservation and the cost of memory maintenance

#### Eirn - The Frostbound Oathlands
- **Magic System:** Endurance-based magic tied to oaths and pacts
- **Cultural Elements:** Clan hearths, ice-carved oaths, endurance rituals
- **Narrative Function:** Tests the durability of commitments under pressure
- **Integration Points:** Oath-endurance trials, etched stone oaths
- **Thematic Role:** Represents commitment and the strength of traditional bonds

#### Sahru - The Sunken Wastes
- **Magic System:** Taboo knowledge and anti-naming practices
- **Cultural Elements:** Communal silence, avoidance of lasting names
- **Narrative Function:** Warns against the dangers of overreaching ambition
- **Integration Points:** Black Sands, Revenants, Name-Bleed phenomenon
- **Thematic Role:** Represents caution and the consequences of hubris

#### Alkyr - The Starward Climb
- **Magic System:** Celestial magic involving aspect communion
- **Cultural Elements:** Listening vigils, pilgrimage practices, star-sight
- **Narrative Function:** Provides cosmic perspective on mortal concerns
- **Integration Points:** Star-Shards, aspect-witness cults, stellar imagery
- **Thematic Role:** Represents transcendence and the search for higher understanding

#### Mirel - The Shrouded Coast
- **Magic System:** Memory-echo magic and mist-weaving
- **Cultural Elements:** Barter storytelling, mist-sellers, veil practices
- **Narrative Function:** Explores the boundary between memory and illusion
- **Integration Points:** Mist-weaving, Return-Echoes, lamp-lighting practices
- **Thematic Role:** Represents uncertainty and the challenge of distinguishing truth from deception

#### Nyth - The Fracture Beyond
- **Magic System:** Conceptual manipulation and anti-ontology magic
- **Cultural Elements:** Disbelief practice, counter-chants, scientific observation
- **Narrative Function:** Challenges fundamental assumptions about reality
- **Integration Points:** Anti-ontology research, concept erosion, geometry-thin visuals
- **Thematic Role:** Represents the limits of understanding and the danger of questioning fundamentals

#### Valen - The Central Plain
- **Magic System:** Trade magic and crossroads manipulation
- **Cultural Elements:** Caravan pledges, cross-trade practices, route negotiations
- **Narrative Function:** Serves as the convergence point for all regional influences
- **Integration Points:** Treaty of Three Stones crossing, caravan rings, crossroads magic
- **Thematic Role:** Represents unity and the coming together of diverse approaches

## Magic System Integration

### Taxonomy Implementation
Each magic type should be visually and mechanically distinct:

- **Spirit (Thalen):** Green-gold prismatic sap shimmer
- **Covenant (Cyris):** Marble light, white fissure lines
- **Engineered (Virek):** Brass sparks and schematic overlays
- **Tidal (Kallum):** Oily iridescence, slow breathing waves
- **Void-Edge (Nyth):** Absence-consuming hues, silence-bloom

### Entropy Mechanics
- Node entropy should accumulate visibly throughout the narrative
- Different actions should contribute different amounts of entropy
- Consequences of entropy should escalate progressively
- The Traveler's activities should create measurable impacts

### The Three Laws of Ascension
- **Law 1 (Irrecoverable Cost):** Each use should permanently remove something from the character
- **Law 2 (Node Entropy):** Each use should increase permanent damage to infrastructure
- **Law 3 (Political Targeting):** Each use should increase attention from institutions
