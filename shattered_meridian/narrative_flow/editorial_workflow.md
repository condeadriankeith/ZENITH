# Editorial Workflow

## Pre-Draft Review Process

### 1. Planning Review
- **Document:** Chapter synopsis and beat sheet
- **Reviewer:** Lead Editor
- **Focus:** Narrative logic, thematic alignment, canon compliance
- **Checklist:**
  - Does the scene advance the plot appropriately?
  - Are canonical elements properly planned for inclusion?
  - Do character arcs develop as intended?
  - Is the thematic content aligned with the theme map?

### 2. Research Verification
- **Document:** Supporting lore and reference materials
- **Reviewer:** Worldbuilding Consultant
- **Focus:** Consistency with established canon
- **Checklist:**
  - Do all magic system applications follow established rules?
  - Are place names spelled correctly and used appropriately?
  - Do character traits align with the character bible?
  - Are canonical phrases preserved verbatim where required?

## Draft Review Process

### 3. First Pass - Content Review
- **Document:** Scene draft
- **Reviewer:** Content Editor
- **Focus:** Storytelling effectiveness, character development, plot advancement
- **Checklist:**
  - Does the scene accomplish its stated goals?
  - Are characters acting consistently with their established nature?
  - Does the scene integrate world-building elements effectively?
  - Are the emotional anchors properly referenced?

### 4. Second Pass - Canon Compliance
- **Document:** Scene draft
- **Reviewer:** Canon Compliance Officer
- **Focus:** Adherence to established lore and constraints
- **Checklist:**
  - Do all canonical phrases appear verbatim where required?
  - Are the Three Laws of Ascension properly respected?
  - Is the Traveler's nameless/left-handed/pack-carrying nature maintained?
  - Do magic system applications follow established rules?

### 5. Third Pass - Technical Review
- **Document:** Scene draft
- **Reviewer:** Technical Editor
- **Focus:** Prose quality, style consistency, technical elements
- **Checklist:**
  - Is the prose cinematic, sensory, and lean without purple prose?
  - Are motifs (left-hand, prismatic imagery) used consistently?
  - Are exposition paragraphs under 400 words?
  - Is the tone consistent with the established style?

## Post-Draft Review Process

### 6. Continuity Check
- **Document:** Scene draft in context of previous work
- **Reviewer:** Continuity Editor
- **Focus:** Consistency with previous scenes and established storylines
- **Checklist:**
  - Does the scene maintain continuity with previous events?
  - Are character developments consistent with their arcs?
  - Do world elements align with established lore?
  - Are there any timeline inconsistencies?

### 7. Final Quality Assurance
- **Document:** Scene draft after all revisions
- **Reviewer:** Senior Editor
- **Focus:** Overall quality, readiness for publication
- **Checklist:**
  - Does the scene meet all requirements from previous reviews?
  - Is the prose polished and engaging?
  - Are all canonical elements properly implemented?
  - Is the scene ready for inclusion in the larger work?

## Approval Gates

### Gate A: Planning Approved
- [ ] Planning Review passed
- [ ] Research Verification passed
- [ ] Scene can proceed to drafting

### Gate B: Draft Complete
- [ ] First Pass - Content Review passed
- [ ] Second Pass - Canon Compliance passed
- [ ] Third Pass - Technical Review passed
- [ ] Scene ready for post-draft review

### Gate C: Publication Ready
- [ ] Continuity Check passed
- [ ] Final Quality Assurance passed
- [ ] All editorial requirements satisfied
- [ ] Scene approved for final inclusion

## Revision Protocol

### Minor Revisions (Editorial Suggestions)
- Scope: Grammar, style, minor factual corrections
- Process: Editor suggests changes, author implements
- Approval: Editor approval sufficient

### Major Revisions (Content Changes)
- Scope: Plot changes, character behavior, thematic elements
- Process: Editor identifies issues, author revises, resubmit for review
- Approval: Full review process required

### Canon Revisions (Lore Changes)
- Scope: Magic system rules, established facts, canonical phrases
- Process: Editor flags conflict, consult with Lead Editor, author revises
- Approval: Lead Editor approval required

## Quality Standards

### Acceptable Quality Indicators
- Prose is cinematic, sensory, and lean
- All canonical elements are properly implemented
- Character behavior is consistent
- World-building is integrated effectively
- Themes are appropriately explored

### Rejectable Issues
- Canon violations without approval
- Poor prose quality (purple prose, etc.)
- Character inconsistency
- Major continuity errors
- Missing required elements

## Expedited Review Process

### Fast-Track Eligibility
- Scenes that have passed all planning reviews
- Authors with proven track record of compliance
- Minor scene revisions (under 20% content change)

### Fast-Track Process
- Skip first pass content review
- Combine canon compliance and technical review
- Streamlined continuity check
- Accelerated approval gate processing

## Escalation Protocol

### Issue Categories
- **Technical:** Style, grammar, formatting
- **Content:** Plot, character, thematic issues
- **Canon:** Lore, continuity, established facts
- **Structural:** Major changes to established elements

### Escalation Triggers
- Repeated issues in the same category
- Conflicts between reviewers
- Novel situations not covered by guidelines
- Disputes between author and reviewer

### Escalation Process
1. Team discussion to resolve issue
2. Lead Editor consultation if needed
3. Canon Committee review for lore disputes
4. Final decision by Senior Editor
