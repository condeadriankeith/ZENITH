# Scene Template

## Header Information
```
# Scene [Number]: [Title]
**Location:** [Specific location in the world]
**POV:** [Point of view character]
**Time:** [Time of day, season, chronology reference]
**Mood/Atmosphere:** [Brief description of the scene's emotional tone]
```

## Scene Opening
- **Setting Grounding:** Open with vivid sensory details that establish the location
- **Character Placement:** Show the POV character's immediate state and situation
- **Hook:** Begin with action, dialogue, or compelling imagery that engages the reader

## Scene Development

### Action/Dialogue Section
- **Goal:** What the character(s) are trying to accomplish in this scene
- **Conflict:** What prevents easy success
- **Progress:** How the situation develops or changes
- **Discovery:** New information or revelation that advances the plot

### World Building Integration
- **Magic System:** Demonstrate appropriate magic taxonomy (Spirit, Covenant, Engineered, Tidal, Void-Edge)
- **Cultural Detail:** Show regional customs, beliefs, or practices
- **Environmental Elements:** Use setting details that reinforce the world's unique qualities
- **Sensory Details:** Include prismatic imagery, left-hand motif, and other recurring elements

### Character Development
- **Internal State:** Show the character's emotional and psychological condition
- **Motivation:** Clarify what drives the character's actions
- **Relationships:** Develop character connections through interaction
- **Growth:** Demonstrate how the character changes or learns

## Scene Conclusion
- **Stakes Elevation:** Increase tension or reveal consequences
- **Transition:** Lead into the next scene or section
- **Cliffhanger/Revelation:** End on a note that compels continued reading

## Quality Checks

### Canon Compliance
- [ ] Canonical phrases appear verbatim where required
- [ ] Magic system rules followed consistently
- [ ] Place names spelled correctly
- [ ] Character traits maintained accurately

### Character Consistency
- [ ] Traveler remains nameless throughout
- [ ] Left-hand motif integrated appropriately
- [ ] Pack of knowledge referenced if relevant
- [ ] Emotional anchors acknowledged where appropriate

### World Building
- [ ] Appropriate magic taxonomy used
- [ ] Regional characteristics reflected
- [ ] Three Laws of Ascension respected
- [ ] Entropy consequences acknowledged

### Prose Quality
- [ ] Cinematic, sensory writing style
- [ ] Lean prose without purple prose
- [ ] Prismatic/oil-on-water imagery included
- [ ] No exposition paragraphs exceed 400 words

### Thematic Integration
- [ ] Primary themes addressed
- [ ] Symbolic elements used appropriately
- [ ] Motifs maintained consistently
- [ ] Philosophical questions engaged

## Post-Scene Notes
- **Next Scene Setup:** How this scene prepares for the following scene
- **Series Arc Progression:** How this advances the overall story
- **Reader Engagement:** How this maintains or increases investment
