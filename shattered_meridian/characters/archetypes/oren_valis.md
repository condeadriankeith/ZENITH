Oren Valis — The Marble Magistrate

Slug: oren_valis.md
Region: Cyris

A high-ranking official of the Cyris Covenant, Oren enforces the rigid social laws of the Marble Dominion with a cold, analytical precision. He views the Traveler as a chaotic variable that must be contained or "indexed" to preserve the city's stability.

Oren is a man of sharp angles and stiff fabrics. He wears the white-and-gold robes of the Magistrate, stiffened with powdered marble to ensure they never wrinkle. His face is perpetually clean-shaven, and his eyes are the color of polished flint. He carries a heavy, iron-bound ledger and a stylus made from a sharpened shard of a Meridian Core. When he speaks, his voice is like the sound of stone sliding against stone.

Oren serves as an initial antagonist and later a complicated ally. He represents the systemic weight of the world’s laws and the desperation of those trying to maintain order in the face of inevitable collapse.

The sound of a heavy quill scratching against thick parchment and the faint smell of ozone and ink that lingers in his wake.

Oren is a man who lives by the law, yet he secretly uses "un-indexed" nodes to power his own private research into the Fracture, breaking the very Covenants he executes others for violating.

- Act I — Ch. 2 (The Gates of Cyris): Oren detains the Traveler, attempting to "index" their name and origin, leading to a tense interrogation where the Traveler first experiences the weight of the Covenant.
- Act II — Ch. 15 (The High Court): Oren is forced to choose between the survival of Cyris and the strict adherence to the Law, eventually allowing the Traveler to escape with a forbidden sigil.
- Act III — Ch. 24 (The Falling Marble): As Cyris begins to crumble, Oren remains at his post, recording the final moments of the city into his ledger, providing the Traveler with the "Legal Key" to the Hollow's outer gate.

The Great Hall of Cyris was a cathedral of silence, the air thick with the scent of incense and the cold, white light of the noon sun. Oren Valis sat behind a desk carved from a single block of translucent marble, his stylus moving with a rhythmic, mechanical precision. He didn't look up when the Traveler was brought before him, his chains rattling against the polished floor.

"Name," Oren said, his voice flat and devoid of emotion. 

"I have none that you would recognize," the Traveler replied.

Oren stopped writing. He looked up, his flint-grey eyes narrowing. "In Cyris, an un-indexed name is a hole in the world. It is a leak in the Meridian, a flaw in the Covenant. You are a variable, Traveler. And variables are... messy." He leaned forward, the white light of the hall reflecting off his robes. "I do not like mess. I like order. I like the weight of the Law. And right now, your existence is weighing very heavily on my patience."

### Supplemental Lore: The Covenants of Cyris

The city of Cyris is built on the belief that reality is a contract. The "Covenants" are a series of complex, metaphysical agreements between the citizens and the local Meridian Node. In exchange for the stability of the land and the predictability of the seasons, the citizens must adhere to a strict set of behavioral and linguistic laws.

The Magistrates are the enforcers of these contracts. They are trained from childhood to perceive the "Friction" caused by law-breaking—a subtle, vibrating heat that emanates from those who violate the Covenant. When a Magistrate "indexes" a person, they are effectively binding that person's identity to the city's ledger, making their every action a matter of public record.

### The Stylus of Valis

Oren’s stylus is a relic from the pre-Fracture era. It is tipped with a "Core-Shard"—a fragment of a destroyed Meridian Core that still retains a fraction of its original authority. This allows Oren to write directly into the "Meridian Lattice" of the city, creating temporary zones of absolute law where gravity, light, and even sound behave according to his dictates. 

1. **The White Ink:** A suspension of powdered marble and node-residue that glows when applied to parchment.
2. **The Iron Ledger:** Bound in cold-iron to prevent its contents from being altered by spirit-influence or name-bleed.
