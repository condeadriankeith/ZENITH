Hella Iron-Thumb — The Marble-Mason

Slug: hella_iron_thumb.md
Region: Cyris

A master mason and structural engineer for the Cyris Covenant, Hella is responsible for the maintenance and repair of the city's massive marble architecture. She is a woman of immense physical strength and quiet focus, her life dedicated to the preservation of the city's stability.

Hella is a broad-shouldered, muscular woman with skin the color of polished marble. She wears a heavy leather apron and a series of iron-bound tools strapped to her belt. Her right thumb has been replaced with a "Mason-Key"—a specialized iron prosthetic used for carving and shaping stone. Her hair is a short, practical crop of white-grey, and her eyes are a sharp, flint-grey. She smells of stone-dust, wet marble, and the sharp, antiseptic scent of node-residue.

Hella serves as a source of structural knowledge and a complicated ally. She provides the Traveler with the "Mason-Keys" needed to access the restricted areas of Cyris and helps them understand the dangerous potential of the city's failing architecture.

The rhythmic *clink-scrape* of her mason-key against the stone and the low, gutteral hum of the marble whenever she is working.

Hella seeks to preserve the stability of Cyris, yet she is haunted by the realization that the very architecture she spends her life repairing is the primary source of the "Friction" that is accelerating the city's decay.

- Act I — Ch. 4 (The Great Wall): Hella saves the Traveler from a "Marble-Slide"—a sudden collapse of the city's architecture—and eventually agrees to repair the Traveler's equipment.
- Act II — Ch. 18 (The High Court): Hella and the Traveler infiltrate the city's central node to retrieve a "Core-Shard," a journey that requires Hella to use her own body as a shield against the falling stone.
- Act III — Ch. 30 (The Final Stone): Hella stays behind to defend the city's main gates, using her mason-key to "anchor" the architecture against the advancing Absence.

The Great Wall of Cyris was a cathedral of white marble, the air thick with the scent of incense and the cold, white light of the noon sun. She held up the finished seal, the white light reflecting in her eyes. "Take it. It's a heavy burden, but a light one won't do you any good where you're going."

### Supplemental Lore: The Marble-Masonry of Cyris

In the white cliffs of Cyris, the architecture is not just a place to live; it is a "Metaphysical Contract." The "Covenants" that govern the city are etched into the marble using specialized tools and node-oil. This process "anchors" the city's laws into the physical world, ensuring that gravity, light, and even sound behave according to the dictates of the Magistrates.

The Marble-Masons are the enforcers of this architecture. They are trained to perceive the "Friction" caused by structural decay—a subtle, vibrating heat that emanates from those blocks that are no longer in sync with the Covenant. By applying different types of "Node-Patches" to these blocks, a mason can restore the city's stability. However, the process is extremely taxing on the mason's mind and body. Constant exposure to the raw energy of the stone can lead to "Marble-Sickness," a condition where the mason's own skin takes on the texture of polished stone, as they literally become part of the city they serve.

### The Mason-Key of Hella

Hella’s thumb prosthetic is more than a tool; it is a "Metaphysical Siphon."

1. **The Stone-Grip:** Provides the pressure needed to carve and shape the hardest marble.
2. **The Covenant-Etcher:** A small chamber in the hilt that stores the "Node-Ink" used to etch the city's laws into the stone.
