The Codex of the First Breath
(To be located in codex/genesis_myth.md)

I. The Age of Silence
Before the mountains breathed, before the oceans knew the taste of salt, there was only the Astral Void. And in the Void, there sat the Celestials.

They were not gods of flesh and blood. They were the Wardens of Entropy. They did not dream; they calculated. To them, existence was an equation, and the preferred result was Silence. Perfect, cold, immutable order.

But among the Wardens, there was a desire to build. To craft a cage for the chaotic energies that drifted between the stars.

II. The Forging of the Shards
To build a world, the Celestials needed tools. They reached into the raw chaos of the Void and refined it into the Five Aspects of Creation. They solidified the abstract into the tangible. These were the World Shards.

Crimson: The Aspect of Body. The pulse, the heat, the iron in the blood.

Azure: The Aspect of Mind. The memory, the logic, the flow of thought.

Veridian: The Aspect of Root. The growth, the decay, the hunger of the wild.

Gold: The Aspect of Law. The gravity, the time, the binding oath.

Violet: The Aspect of Void. The magic, the entropy, the space between atoms.

Using these Shards, Val-Kora, the Star-Warden, hammered the nebula into a sphere. He named it Aethelgard.

III. The Garden and the Watchers
Aethelgard was perfect because it was obedient. The mountains did not move unless commanded. The tides bowed to the moon.

To maintain this engine, the Celestials breathed life into dust, creating the Mortals. We were not designed to rule. We were designed to be custodians. Gardeners. We were given strong backs to till the earth and short lives so we would not ask questions.

The Celestials retreated to the Zenith—the Crown of the World—to watch. They left us one commandment, etched into the foundation of the Star-Altar:

"Mortals should not meddle with the power of the Gods."

IV. The Theft of Fire (The First Era)
For a thousand years, there was peace. But the Shards, though hidden deep within the earth, sang to the mortals. The Violet Aspect whispered of power. The Azure Aspect whispered of knowledge.

The first scholars dug deep. They did not just mine ore; they mined the Code of Creation. They touched the Shards.

The moment skin touched the Shard, the Name-Bleed began. Mortals traded their memories for power. They built floating cities. They extended their lives. They rewrote the laws of gravity. They stopped being gardeners and tried to become Architects.

V. The Great Filter
From the Zenith, Val-Kora looked down. He did not see progress. He saw infection. The equation was unbalanced.

The Celestials did not send a plague. They simply turned the key.

They initiated The Great Filter. The sky opened, and star-fire rained upon the floating cities. The Shards were ripped from the earth, shattering the continents. The civilization of the First Era was not just destroyed; it was erased. The history books were burned. The names were forgotten.

The world was reset to zero.

VI. The Current Age (The Cycle of Dust)
The Celestials returned the Shards to the deep vaults, locking them away. They left the survivors in the mud, traumatized and ignorant.

Centuries passed. We forgot the floating cities. We forgot the Filter. We built new kingdoms—The Magistrates of Meridian, the Virek of the Rust Sprawl. We told ourselves that the ruins were just myths.

But the Shards are eternal. They cannot be destroyed, only buried. And the earth shifts.

Now, the vaults are cracking. The song of the Shards is rising again. The Magistrates enforce the Law of Silence to keep us safe, to keep the Celestials asleep. But the Virek dig for power, and the Zealots pray for the end.

The Equation is drifting again. Val-Kora is watching from the Zenith, his hand hovering over the reset button.

We are living on borrowed time.

# The First Breath: A Monologue

Before the mountains breathed... before the oceans knew the taste of salt... there was only the Math.

Cold. Perfect. Silent.

The Celestials did not dream. They calculated. To them, existence was an equation, and the preferred result was silence. But in the void, they found a flaw. A hunger. And to fix the Void... they had to break it.

They forged the Shards.
**Crimson**... for the blood that beats.
**Azure**... for the mind that remembers.
**Veridian**... for the root that hungers.
**Violet**... for the chaos that binds.
And **Gold**... to hold the cage together.

They built Aethelgard. Not a paradise... but an engine. And they made *us*. The gardeners. The custodians. We were given strong backs to till the earth... and short lives, so we would not ask questions.

They left us with one law. A single line of code etched into the roof of the world:
*"Mortals should not meddle with the power of the Gods."*

But we were curious.

We dug too deep. We found the Shards buried in the dark. We touched the fire... and it touched us back. We traded our memories for magic. We traded our names for power. We thought we had become Gods.

But to the Celestials... we were just an error in the equation. And errors must be corrected.

They didn't declare war. They simply turned the key.
Civilization was wiped clean. History was burned. The world was reset to zero.

We forgot the cities. We forgot the fire.
But the Shards never sleep. They are singing again beneath our feet. And in the stars... the Wardens are watching.

The cycle is ending.
And this time... we will not go quietly.
